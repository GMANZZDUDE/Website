Field notes

Instruments

  • Threads draws faint lines between your last eight clicks—temporary constellations of your own making.
  • Calm mode stills parallax, meteors, and nebula drift. Whispers pause until you return.
  • Type drift in order (anywhere, no input focused) for a meteor shower.

Odd truths

  • Sunlight reaches Earth in about eight minutes. The Milky Way’s center is roughly 26,000 light-years away—so that light began before recorded history.
  • Many “stars” are binaries or whole crowds; our single Sun is almost lonely by comparison.
  • The sky you see is a slice through time: nearby stars are nearly “now,” distant ones are deep postcards.
  • Meteors glow from air compressed ahead of a grain of dust—not from friction alone.
